About Chandigarh

India's First Planned City

Chandigarh, meaning "The Fort of Chandi" (named after the nearby Chandi Mandir temple), stands as a testament to modernist urban planning and architectural excellence. Designed by the legendary Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier in 1950, commissioned by India's first Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru, this city was envisioned as a symbol of India's freedom and faith in the future.

After India's partition in 1947, Chandigarh was built to serve as the capital for East Punjab and to resettle refugees from West Punjab. The foundation stone was laid in 1952, and by 1966, it became a Union Territory serving as the capital for both Punjab and Haryana states. Covering 114 sq km at the foothills of the Shivalik hills, Chandigarh is home to over 1 million residents with an impressive literacy rate of 86%.

Le Corbusier's revolutionary design incorporated his innovative "Modulor" system for human scale, multilayered circulation systems, and residential sectors that perfectly blend concrete with greenery. The Capitol Complex, featuring the High Court, Legislative Assembly, and Secretariat built with rough concrete and geometric forms, earned UNESCO World Heritage status for its architectural significance.

Known as the "City Beautiful", Chandigarh pioneered the Green City Concept with well-planned landscaping, extensive tree plantations, open spaces, and green belts. The city's design includes well-wooded peripheral forests, the scenic Sukhna Lake, and a protected Wildlife Sanctuary, creating a perfect harmony between urban development and nature.

Travel Information

Everything you need to know to make your Chandigarh visit smooth and memorable.

Best Time to Visit

October to March for pleasant weather. Avoid summer months (May-July) when temperatures exceed 40°C.

Weather

Winter: 5-20°C, Summer: 25-45°C, Monsoon: July-September. Pack layers for winter evenings.

Language

Hindi, Punjabi, and English widely spoken. Most signs and directions available in English.

Getting There

By Air

Chandigarh International Airport is 11 km from city center. Well-connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore.

30 min drive to city

By Train

Chandigarh Railway Station connects to major cities. Delhi trains run frequently throughout the day.

3-4 hours from Delhi

By Road

National Highway connects to Delhi (260 km), Shimla (117 km), and Amritsar (230 km).

4-5 hours from Delhi

Getting Around

Auto-rickshaws

Metered and convenient for short distances. Available throughout the city.

Negotiate fare before starting or insist on meter.

Local Buses

CTU (Chandigarh Transport Undertaking) operates extensive bus network.

Affordable and connects all sectors. Get a route map from tourist office.

Cycle Rentals

Public bike-sharing system available. Perfect for exploring the well-planned sectors.

Download Hexi app for bike rentals at 180+ docking stations.

Taxi & Ride-sharing

Ola and Uber available. Pre-paid taxis at airport and railway station.

Book in advance during peak tourist season.
